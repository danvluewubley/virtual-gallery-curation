The Starry Night by Vincent van Gogh

The Starry Night depicts a dreamy interpretation of the artist's asylum room's sweeping view of Saint-Rémy-de-Provence at night, although it was painted from memory during the day. The swirling night sky, the bright crescent moon, and the luminous stars are juxtaposed against a quiet village and a massive, dark cypress tree in the foreground.

Fun Fact!

Van Gogh painted The Starry Night during his stay at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, where he was admitted after a mental health crisis. The painting is often seen as a reflection of his turbulent state of mind.

The Last Supper by Leonardo da Vinci

The Last Supper depicts the moment Jesus announces that one of his twelve disciples will betray him. The painting captures the dramatic reaction of each apostle, showcasing Leonardo's mastery of human emotion and perspective. Jesus sits at the center of the long table, with his disciples grouped in threes on either side.

Fun Fact!

Due to Leonardo's experimental technique and the conditions in the refectory, the painting began to deteriorate soon after its completion. Today, only a fraction of Leonardo's original work remains, with much of the surface having been restored or repainted.

The Scream by Edvard Munch

The Scream is one of the most iconic images in art history, capturing a figure with a horrified expression standing on a bridge against a tumultuous sky. The swirling sky and the figure's contorted face convey a profound sense of existential angst and despair. The painting is often seen as a symbol of modern human anxiety and existential dread.

Fun Fact!

The Scream has had a lasting impact on popular culture, inspiring parodies, references, and adaptations in various media, including films, cartoons, and advertisements. The iconic image has become a symbol of modern human anxiety.
